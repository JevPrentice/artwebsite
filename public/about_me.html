About Me

The primary aim in my portraits is to explore what is unseen or unfamiliar in the faces we present to the world. Qualities, feelings, transitions, disappointments, life experiences, dreams and desires are what energize my portraits – all the more so when polarities can be bridged in a single work. My focus is on getting the work to feel like the subject, rather than look like the subject.

I am drawn to combine media in an assortment of ways. Collage, charcoal and acrylic or oil on canvas, watercolour, acrylic and charcoal on paper – wet and dry media juxtaposed and overlaid as a way to hold and embrace contradictions in my subject. I find the varied, experimental surfaces create a sense of aliveness, vibrancy and depth. These are tools to lead me intuitively to discoveries in the process of each portrait.

Inward reflection, honesty and courage are required on all of our journeys into knowing ourselves fully. My work is about facing ourselves and embracing ourselves in this way; facing and embracing all of who we are.

I studied Fine Art at the Cape Technikon, my interest in portraiture beginning through an introspective process of self-portraits. I am now a professional portrait artist, working on a commission basis. I live with my partner and son in Cape Town.
